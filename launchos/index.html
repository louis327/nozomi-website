FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Nozomi LaunchOS different from an accelerator or launchpad?

+

We're not a launchpad or a traditional accelerator.

Unlike most accelerators, we don't take on early-stage teams still figuring things out. We only work with later-stage projects that already have a product, a strong team, real traction, and are in the final stretch before going public with their token.

And unlike launchpads that only show up for the token sale and disappear right after, we stay with you through the entire process. We don't just help you oversubscribe a round — we help you design the token, raise from your community on fair terms, and then guide you through the full TGE process.

Why is this important / why are we doing this?

+

Because right now, most token launches are broken.

Founders spend years building, only to get stuck in a system designed for VCs — and not for them, and definitely not for their communities. Projects raise at inflated valuations, give insiders early exits, and launch at billion-dollar FDVs where there's no upside left for users.

We built Nozomi LaunchOS to fix that. To give founders a structured, fair way to go public with aligned capital, clear narrative, and real community ownership from day one.

Who is this program for?

+

LaunchOS is built for founders who already have a live product or testnet. It's best suited for teams that have already raised a pre-seed and are preparing to close their final round but want to bring their community in alongside VCs.

We prioritize founders who want the community at the same table as VCs — creating real alignment from day one.

Beyond introductions, will you help us negotiate deals?

+

Yes. Getting market makers and exchanges isn't the hard part. If you've got enough money, you'll always get a deal. The problem is the terms. That's where most founders get screwed.

Our team joins those conversations with you, helping navigate the details, push back where it matters, and negotiate fair, sustainable terms so your launch is built to last.

Does Nozomi invest directly in projects?

+

Nozomi doesn't invest directly or operate as a traditional fund. Instead, we invest alongside the community through our Launchpad.

We don't lead rounds or take control positions. Our role is to help structure fair raises, bring in high-quality community investors, and give founders access to strategic capital that believes in the long-term success of the project — not just the token event.

What does it cost?

+

There are no upfront fees. Our motto is simple — we deserve nothing unless we get you results.

Nozomi operates on a KPI and success-aligned model, meaning we only succeed when you do. All terms are discussed transparently during onboarding.

How long is the program?

+

LaunchOS runs across three core phases — typically over 8–12 weeks, depending on how close your project is to TGE.

Typical timeline: Plan (1–3 weeks), Raise (4 weeks), Launch (4+ weeks). Every team gets a customized roadmap based on where they are and what's left to get done.

How much time do founders need to commit?

+

Every team will have different needs — we adapt to you. On the low end, expect weekly calls and ongoing collaboration with the Nozomi team. This is an execution-heavy program, not a passive advisory.

How do you work with L1/L2 ecosystems and accelerators?

+

We're not here to replace existing ecosystems or accelerator programs. We work closely with many leading L1s, L2s, and incubators around the world.

Once a project graduates from their early-stage program, Nozomi can step in to guide them through the final stretch to market. Think of us as the bridge between acceleration and launch.

Is this program chain-specific?

+

No. LaunchOS supports teams across all ecosystems, though we often collaborate with key networks and partners for ecosystem-specific initiatives.

How do I apply?

+

Submit a short application with your project details, team background, and TGE timeline. If shortlisted, you'll be invited to a review call with the Nozomi team. Selected teams are then onboarded into the three-phase program.
