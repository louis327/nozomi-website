The problem

If any of this sounds familiar,
this course was built for you.

Not generic startup advice. The specific things killing Web3 raises right now.

01
You have had 20 or 30 meetings and nothing has committed
Everyone says they love it. Nobody writes a cheque. You keep booking more meetings hoping the next one will be different. The round quietly dies with no clear reason why.
02
Investors keep asking for traction you do not have yet
You are pre-product or early MVP. The product is real and the vision is clear. But every meeting ends with "come back when you have more." You do not know what would actually change that answer.
03
Your valuation feels right but investors keep pushing back on it
You picked a number that felt credible. Now every meeting turns into a negotiation about price instead of a conversation about the opportunity. You cannot defend it without just repeating it louder.
04
Your deck explains the tech perfectly and investors still are not excited
You spent months on the architecture. The deck covers every feature in detail. Investors nod through the whole thing and never follow up. The product is real. The pitch is not landing.
05
You know you need community capital but have no idea how to do it right
Everyone tells you to raise from your community. Nobody tells you how to structure it without creating a wall of sell pressure from people who just wanted a quick 2x.
06
You are raising right now and nothing from the bull run playbook works
The bar is completely different. What got a term sheet in 2021 gets a polite pass today. You are not sure if the problem is the market, the story, or the terms. It is probably all three.

What is inside

9 modules. A complete system.
From first number to final wire.

Every module builds on the last. Nothing is standalone because nothing in fundraising is standalone.

1
Fundraising goals and blueprint
Get clear on your fundraising goals. How much to raise, what it is for, and what milestone it funds. Set your target, build the number bottom-up line by line, allocate it across four buckets, and write the one-sentence raise narrative you will use in every investor conversation.
2
Valuation and deal structure
How Web3 valuations actually work. The inflation trap that kills rounds before they start. SAFEs vs SAFTs. Vesting mechanics that keep investors aligned instead of waiting for the cliff.
3
Choose your fundraising model
VC, ICO 2.0, IDO, IEO, grants, hybrid stack. Which one fits your stage and community, and the community raise timing problem most founders get badly wrong.
4
Messaging stack and elevator pitch
Positioning statement, USP, hell and heaven framework. A pitch deliverable in 10 seconds or 90, written and rehearsed before you approach anyone.
5
Build your pitch deck
The three questions every investor is answering in the first two minutes. Every slide built for your actual project. The clarity test that exposes every vague slide before an investor does.
6
Advisors: build a team investors bet on
Your advisory board is your first fundraising test. If credible people will not back you with their time, what makes you think a VC will back you with capital?
7
Build your investor list
Three filters. Four investor categories. Three tiers. The warm path system. A CRM built for momentum, not just storing names.
8-9
Outreach, the sprint, and close
The rolling raise trap. The sprint model. Running meetings. Six objections with the right answers. Building FOMO. From verbal yes to signed documents to wire.
